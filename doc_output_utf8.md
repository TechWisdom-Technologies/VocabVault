__VocabVault ÔÇö Product Specification Document__

__by TechWisdom Technologies__

__Overview__

VocabVault is a web and mobile SaaS application designed for English vocabulary mastery and IELTS preparation\. It is not a flashcard app\. It is not a quiz game\. It is a structured, science\-backed word acquisition system that takes a learner through ten cognitive stages per word ÔÇö from first exposure to fluent spoken production ÔÇö before that word is considered truly learned\.

The application is built on a single conviction: vocabulary is not memorized, it is internalized\. Seeing a word is not the same as knowing it\. Typing a definition is not the same as using it\. VocabVault closes the gap between passive recognition and active command ÔÇö the gap that separates intermediate learners from fluent ones\.

__The Homepage__

When a visitor arrives at the VocabVault link for the first time, they land on a fully designed product homepage ÔÇö not a login screen, not a blank page, but a living showcase of what the platform is and why it exists\.

The homepage communicates the product's identity: what VocabVault is, who it is for, what problem it solves, and why it works better than alternatives\. It features a clear breakdown of the ten\-stage learning system, testimonials from learners who improved their IELTS scores, a feature comparison against conventional vocabulary apps, documentation and guides for new users, pricing and plan information, and a prominent __Get Started__ button\.

That button leads to the authentication page\.

__Authentication & Onboarding__

__Login & Sign\-Up__

If the visitor already has an account, they log in directly\. If not, they register with their email address and complete email verification before gaining access\. No unverified account proceeds past this step\.

__First\-Time Account Setup__

On their very first login, new users are taken through an onboarding flow before reaching the main dashboard\. This is mandatory and cannot be skipped\. During setup, the user provides:

- Full name
- Profile picture
- Date of birth
- Age
- Nationality
- Phone number
- Profession
- Their primary reason for using VocabVault \(IELTS preparation, general English improvement, academic vocabulary, professional communication, etc\.\)

This information personalizes the experience and is used for progress analytics and dashboard customization\.

__Session Resumption__

If a learner exits the application mid\-session ÔÇö at any point during a word cycle ÔÇö the system saves their exact position\. When they return, they resume from precisely where they left off\. Nothing is reset\. No progress is lost\.

__The Dashboard__

After completing onboarding \(or on every subsequent login\), the learner arrives at their main dashboard\.

On the very first visit, a popup appears containing the full rules of the platform ÔÇö how the stage system works, how scoring operates, what unlocks the next word, and what happens when a stage is failed\. This popup must be acknowledged before the dashboard becomes interactive\.

The dashboard is the learner's command center\. It is organized into clearly defined sections, giving the learner complete visibility into their progress, performance, and standing at all times\.

__Word Queue__

The daily word set is displayed prominently\. The current word is shown as active and accessible\. The remaining four words of the day are shown as locked ÔÇö each one unlocking sequentially only after the previous word is completed and passed\. The learner always knows exactly where they stand in their daily set\.

__Daily & Streak Information__

- __Day Count__ ÔÇö the total number of days the learner has been active on VocabVault since joining
- __Current Streak__ ÔÇö consecutive days of unbroken learning activity
- __Daily Word Progress__ ÔÇö how many of today's 5 words have been completed

__Progress Overview__

- __Overall Progress \(All Time\)__ ÔÇö a cumulative view of every word ever completed since the learner joined, shown as a progress curve over time
- __Overall Progress \(By Day\)__ ÔÇö a day\-by\-day breakdown of how many words were completed on each calendar day, visualized as a calendar view the learner can scroll through
- __Total Words Learned__ ÔÇö the exact count of all words fully completed and passed to date

__Scoring & Performance Analytics__

- __Total Score \(All Time\)__ ÔÇö the sum of all points earned across every word and every stage ever completed
- __Today's Score__ ÔÇö points earned within the current calendar day
- __Day Average Score__ ÔÇö the learner's average score per word per day, calculated across all active days
- __Average Score Per Stage__ ÔÇö a breakdown showing the learner's average performance on each of the 10 stages individually ÔÇö so they can see exactly which stages they consistently excel at and which ones they need to improve
- __Average Time Per Stage__ ÔÇö how long the learner typically spends on each of the 10 stages, averaged across all completed words
- __Average Total Time Per Day__ ÔÇö the average amount of time spent completing a full set of 5 words per day, giving the learner a realistic picture of their daily time investment

__Leaderboard__

A global and friend\-based leaderboard showing rankings by total score, words learned, or current streak\. Learners can see where they stand against all VocabVault users or filter to compare only with people they follow\. The leaderboard updates in real time\.

__Recent Activity__

A chronological activity feed showing the learner's most recent actions ÔÇö words completed, stages retaken, scores earned, streaks maintained, and milestones reached\. This gives the learner an at\-a\-glance record of what they have done recently without needing to dig through their full history\.

__Word History__

Every word ever completed is stored and accessible\. Clicking any word opens its full stage\-by\-stage breakdown ÔÇö individual scores, time spent per stage, and whether any stages were retaken\.

__Profile Settings__

The learner can access and update their profile at any time from the dashboard ÔÇö name, profile picture, date of birth, nationality, phone number, profession, learning goals, notification preferences, and account security settings\.

__Free / Pro Plan Indicator__

The dashboard clearly displays the learner's current plan ÔÇö Free or Pro ÔÇö with a visible indicator\. Free plan users can see what features and capabilities are available to them versus what is unlocked on the Pro plan, with a clear and non\-intrusive upgrade prompt\.

__Stage Instructions, Marking Guide & Tips__

Accessible at all times from the dashboard and from within every stage, this section gives learners complete transparency into how the system works, how they are being evaluated, and how to perform at their best\.

__How to Access__

A dedicated __How It Works__ button is present on the dashboard and as a persistent icon within every active stage\. Tapping it at any point opens the instructions panel without interrupting the session or resetting any timers\.

__What Is Covered__

__Stage\-by\-Stage Instructions__ A plain\-language explanation of what each stage requires, what the learner must do, and what conditions must be met before progressing\. No ambiguity ÔÇö the learner knows exactly what is expected of them before they begin\.

__Marking Criteria__ A transparent breakdown of how points are awarded in every stage ÔÇö which stages are scored by timer completion, which are scored by AI evaluation, which are scored by quiz accuracy, and exactly what the AI looks for in spoken and written submissions\. The learner is never guessing at how their work is being judged\.

__Pass/Fail Rules__ A clear explanation of the 80\-point threshold, what triggers a stage retry, and how the retry process works\. Learners understand that failing a word does not mean starting over ÔÇö it means returning only to the specific stages where they fell short\.

__Tips Per Stage__ Practical, specific advice for performing well in each stage ÔÇö how to approach the pronunciation repetitions in Stage 1, how to speak naturally rather than robotically in Stage 2, how to read actively rather than passively in Stage 5, how to avoid mechanical repetition in Stage 9, and how to use the target word fluently rather than forcibly in Stage 10\.

__AI Evaluation Guide__ An honest explanation of what the AI is listening and looking for in Stages 2, 9, and 10 ÔÇö pronunciation accuracy, grammatical correctness, contextual appropriateness, and natural fluency\. This helps learners understand that the system rewards genuine command of the word, not performance tricks\.

__Common Mistakes__ A list of the most frequent errors learners make at each stage, written to help learners self\-correct before they submit ÔÇö not after\.

__The Daily Word Limit__

A learner completes a maximum of __5 words per day__\. Not 6\. Not 10\. Five\. This is a hard system limit, not a recommendation\.

This constraint is intentional\. Vocabulary acquisition research consistently shows that deep processing of a small number of words produces dramatically better long\-term retention than shallow processing of many words\. Five words done properly ÔÇö taken through all ten stages ÔÇö is more valuable than twenty words skimmed through a review deck\.

The first word in the daily set is available immediately\. Each subsequent word unlocks only after the previous one is completed and passed\.

__The Ten\-Stage Word Cycle__

Every word in VocabVault passes through ten sequential stages\. Stages cannot be skipped, reordered, or bypassed\. Each stage targets a specific cognitive process\. Together, they move the learner from zero exposure to full fluency with a single word\.

Each stage is worth __10 points__\. The total per word is __100 points__\.

__Stage 1 ÔÇö Word Briefing__

__Timer: 45 seconds \(mandatory\)__

The learner sees the word for the first time\. The screen presents everything at once: the phonetic spelling of the word, an audio button for pronunciation, the part of speech, all relevant tense forms, a plain\-English definition, and a second audio button that reads the definition aloud\.

Two hard unlock conditions must both be satisfied before the Next button activates:

1. The pronunciation audio button must be pressed __at least 3 times__ ÔÇö the button visually tracks progress \(1 of 3 ÔåÆ 2 of 3 ÔåÆ 3 of 3\)
2. The learner must remain on the stage for the full __45 seconds__

Neither the audio condition nor the timer condition alone is sufficient\. Both must be met simultaneously\. Completing both awards the full 10 points\.

__Stage 2 ÔÇö Sentence Immersion__

__Timer: 3 minutes__

Six sentences appear on screen, each using the target word in a different tense and context ÔÇö simple present, simple past, future, present perfect, conditional, and continuous usage\. The learner reads each sentence, then presses and holds a record button to speak it aloud\.

The AI evaluates every recording for pronunciation accuracy, fluency, and natural delivery\. Based on combined performance across all six sentences, a score from 0 to 10 is assigned\. All six sentences must be recorded before submission is allowed\.

__Stage 3 ÔÇö Synonym & Antonym Map__

__Timer: 60 seconds \(mandatory\)__

The screen displays __3 synonyms__ and __3 antonyms__ of the target word\. Each entry includes one example sentence showing it in use\. The learner reads through all six entries for the full 60 seconds\. There is no quiz, no clicking, no interaction required\.

The countdown is displayed directly on the Next button\. Completing the timer awards the full 10 points automatically\. The quiz that tests this content comes in Stage 6 ÔÇö this stage is the planting phase, Stage 6 is the harvest\.

__Stage 4 ÔÇö Active Recall Test I__

__Timer: 3 minutes__

A mixed quiz testing retention from Stages 1, 2, and 3:

- __4 Fill\-in\-the\-blank__ questions ÔÇö sentences with a gap where the target word belongs
- __4 Multiple choice__ questions ÔÇö testing definition and usage
- __2 True/False__ statements ÔÇö testing understanding of the word's meaning and application

Every question must be answered before submission is permitted\. Partial submission is blocked\. Points scale directly with correct answers toward the full 10\. Scoring is instant on submission\.

If a learner scores below 8 here, the system flags this stage\. After the word cycle is completed, they will be sent back to this stage specifically before the word is marked complete\.

__Stage 5 ÔÇö Article Deep Read__

__Timer: 300 seconds \(5 minutes, mandatory\)__

Three short articles are presented ÔÇö each approximately __50 words__ ÔÇö drawn from news writing, academic sources, or structured essays\. The target word appears naturally within each article\. There is no audio\. There is no quiz\. The learner reads all three articles carefully and actively\.

The full 300 seconds must pass before the Next button activates\. This timer is enforced without exception\. Completing the timer awards the full 10 points\.

The timer on this stage is the longest in the word cycle by design\. Extended reading in authentic context is where vocabulary transitions from short\-term to long\-term memory\. The system will not allow this stage to be rushed\.

__Stage 6 ÔÇö Active Recall Test II__

__Timer: 3 minutes__

This stage has two parts:

__Part A ÔÇö Spelling:__ The target word is completely removed from the screen\. No reference, no hint\. The learner types the word correctly from memory ÔÇö __3 times in a row__\.

__Part B ÔÇö Matching:__ The learner matches __5__ synonyms or definitions to the correct words from a scrambled list, drawing on what they absorbed in Stage 3\.

Points are calculated from the combined accuracy of both parts\. Completing the stage within the timer and with strong accuracy awards the full 10 points\.

__Stage 7 ÔÇö Active Listening__

__Timer: Up to 5 minutes total__

Three separate audio clips play, each __45 seconds__ in length\. The clips are sourced from lectures, podcast segments, or structured conversations ÔÇö each recorded in a __different English accent__ \(for example: British, American, and Australian\)\. In each clip, the target word is used naturally __2 to 3 times__\.

After each clip, the learner submits how many times they heard the target word\. They answer three separate count fields ÔÇö one per clip\.

Scoring is based on accuracy across all three answers\. An exact count on all three clips earns the full 10 points\. Partial credit is awarded based on how close the answers are to the correct counts\.

__Stage 8 ÔÇö Paragraph Analysis__

__Timer: 5 minutes__

A single paragraph of __200 or more words__ is displayed\. The target word appears in the paragraph multiple times, in potentially different grammatical forms ÔÇö noun, verb, adjective ÔÇö as do some of its synonyms and antonyms from Stage 3\.

The learner submits three separate counts:

1. How many times the __target word__ appeared \(in any form\)
2. How many times a __synonym__ from Stage 3 appeared
3. How many times an __antonym__ from Stage 3 appeared

Points are awarded based on accuracy across all three counts\. Getting all three correct earns the full 10 points\.

__Stage 9 ÔÇö Free Writing__

__Timer: 10 minutes__

The learner writes an original paragraph ÔÇö no template, no sentence starters, no topic assigned\. The system enforces a minimum word count, and the target word must appear __at least 3 times__ within the paragraph\.

The AI evaluates the submission on three criteria: grammatical correctness of each instance of the target word, naturalness and appropriateness of usage in context, and variety across the three or more usages ÔÇö different sentence structures, different positions, different functions\.

Mechanical or robotic insertion will not score well\. Writing the word three times in near\-identical sentences ÔÇö "The word is useful\. The word is important\. The word is good\." ÔÇö will be identified and scored accordingly\. The AI evaluates genuine usage, not mechanical repetition\. Scoring from 0 to 10 reflects this\.

__Stage 10 ÔÇö Spoken Performance__

__Timer: 1 minute__

The learner presses a mic button and speaks for __1 minute__\. They may speak about anything ÔÇö a personal story, an opinion, a description of their day ÔÇö but the target word must appear __at least 3 times__ in naturally flowing, grammatically correct sentences\.

After submission, the AI transcribes the recording, locates every instance of the target word, verifies grammatical and contextual correctness of each usage, and evaluates the overall fluency and naturalness of the full speech\. Robotic or rehearsed delivery is penalized\. Natural, fluent speech with correct usage earns the full 10 points\.

This is the hardest stage in the word cycle\. It requires fluency under pressure with no written reference available\. A learner who completes Stage 10 with a strong score has not studied the word ÔÇö they have acquired it\.

__The Pass/Fail Logic__

Each of the 10 stages is worth 10 points\. The total is __100 points per word__\. A learner must reach __80 points or above__ to pass the word and unlock the next one in their daily set\.

If they fall below 80, the system identifies every stage where they scored below 8 out of 10\. The learner is returned __only to those specific stages__ ÔÇö not the full word cycle\. They retake the flagged stages, recover the missing points, and the word is marked complete\.

This design respects the learner's time\. If someone scored perfectly on 9 stages but rushed Stage 4 and scored a 6, they redo Stage 4 only\. They do not repeat content they have already mastered\.

	

__VocabVault ÔÇö Full Stack Technical Architecture__

__Core Framework__

__Next\.js \(Full Stack\)__ ÔÇö App Router, server components, API routes, and middleware all handled within a single Next\.js project\. Multipage architecture with route groups for public pages, auth pages, onboarding, dashboard, and the word cycle stages\.

__Database & ORM__

__Supabase \(PostgreSQL\)__ ÔÇö Primary database\. Handles all relational data: users, words, stages, scores, progress records, leaderboard entries, activity logs, and plan information\.

__Prisma ORM__ ÔÇö Schema definition, migrations, and all database queries go through Prisma\. Clean type\-safe access to Supabase PostgreSQL from Next\.js server\-side code\.

__Authentication__

__Firebase Authentication__ ÔÇö Handles the actual auth flow: email/password sign\-up, email verification, and session tokens\. Firebase issues the JWT\.

__Supabase Third\-Party Auth__ ÔÇö Firebase JWT is passed into Supabase so that Row Level Security policies on the database recognize the authenticated user\. Both systems stay in sync ÔÇö Firebase owns the identity, Supabase owns the data access control\.

__File Storage__

__Supabase Storage Buckets__ ÔÇö Profile pictures, audio clips for Stage 7, article content assets, and any other static or user\-generated files are stored in Supabase buckets with proper access policies per user\.

__Validation__

__Zod__ ÔÇö Schema validation on all API route inputs, form submissions, onboarding data, quiz answers, and any data coming from the client before it touches the database\.

__Suggested Additional Technologies__

__TypeScript__ ÔÇö Non\-negotiable for a project of this complexity\. Every model, API response, stage state, and score object should be fully typed\. Zod schemas double as TypeScript type sources via z\.infer\.

__TailwindCSS__ ÔÇö Utility\-first styling\. Fast to build, easy to maintain, and pairs cleanly with Next\.js App Router component structure\. Good for building the stage UI, dashboard panels, and progress indicators\.

__shadcn/ui__ ÔÇö Pre\-built accessible component library built on top of Tailwind\. Use for modals, buttons, progress bars, form inputs, tabs, and tooltips throughout the dashboard and stage screens\. Saves significant UI development time without locking you into a heavy component framework\.

__Zustand__ ÔÇö Lightweight client\-side state management\. Use it to manage stage session state ÔÇö current stage, timer countdowns, audio play counts, recording status, answers in progress ÔÇö without lifting everything into server components or prop\-drilling through deep component trees\.

__React Hook Form__ ÔÇö Pairs with Zod for all forms: onboarding setup, login, sign\-up, quiz answers in Stage 4 and Stage 6, count submissions in Stages 7 and 8, and the free writing submission in Stage 9\. Clean, performant, and integrates with Zod resolvers natively\.

__AI __

__Google Gemini API__ ÔÇö Strong alternative to GPT\-4o for text evaluation in Stage 9 and Stage 10\. Gemini 1\.5 Flash is significantly cheaper than GPT\-4o and performs well on grammar and fluency evaluation tasks

__Groq__ ÔÇö Extremely fast inference for LLM tasks\. Uses open models like LLaMA 3 and Mixtral\. Useful for lower\-stakes evaluations where speed matters more than peak accuracy\. Free tier is genuinely useful for early\-stage development\. 

__Deepgram__ ÔÇö Speech\-to\-text transcription\. Strong alternative to OpenAI Whisper for transcribing Stage 2 and Stage 10 recordings\. Faster, cheaper per minute, and has a real free tier\. 

__Vercel__ ÔÇö Deployment platform\. Native Next\.js support, edge functions, automatic preview deployments per branch, and built\-in analytics\. The natural and most seamless choice for a Next\.js SaaS product\.

__Upstash Redis__ ÔÇö Use for rate limiting API routes \(preventing abuse on quiz submission and audio evaluation endpoints\), caching leaderboard data, and managing daily word limit enforcement server\-side so it cannot be bypassed from the client\.

__Resend__ ÔÇö Transactional email service for sending email verification links, welcome emails after onboarding, daily streak reminder notifications, and plan upgrade confirmations\. Clean API, excellent Next\.js integration, and reliable deliverability\.

__Stripe__ ÔÇö Payment processing for the Free/Pro plan upgrade flow\. Handles subscription billing, webhooks to update plan status in the database, and the customer portal for managing or cancelling subscriptions\. Supabase stores the current plan status per user, updated via Stripe webhook on every billing event\.

__Sentry__ ÔÇö Error monitoring and performance tracking in production\. Captures frontend and server\-side errors, slow API routes, and failed stage submissions so bugs are caught before users report them\.

__*Free / Pro Plan Structure*__

*VocabVault operates on a two\-tier access model ÔÇö a Free plan and a Pro plan\. The distinction between the two is simple, permanent, and built around a single boundary: *__*word access*__*\.*

__*Free Plan*__

*The Free plan is available to every user immediately upon sign\-up with no payment required\. It gives the learner full, unrestricted access to the first *__*25 words*__* in the VocabVault library\.*

*All ten stages are fully available on every free word\. The scoring system, pass/fail logic, stage retries, dashboard analytics, leaderboard, streaks, and every feature of the platform works exactly the same on the Free plan as it does on Pro\. There is no degraded experience, no stage restrictions, and no watermarked features\.*

*The Free plan exists to give every learner a genuine, complete experience of what VocabVault is ÔÇö not a teaser, not a demo\. Twenty\-five words taken through all ten stages is a meaningful amount of learning\. A learner who completes all 25 free words has genuinely acquired 25 words\. That is the point\.*

*Once the 25th word is completed, the library locks\. No further words are accessible until the learner upgrades\.*

__*Pro Plan*__

*The Pro plan is a *__*one\-time payment*__* that unlocks lifetime access to the complete VocabVault word library ÔÇö every word currently in the platform and every word added in the future\.*

*There is no monthly subscription\. There is no renewal\. The learner pays once and owns their access permanently\.*

*What Pro unlocks:*

- *Full access to the entire word library ÔÇö no cap, no ceiling*
- *Every future word added to the library included automatically*
- *All existing dashboard features, analytics, leaderboard standing, and stage functionality remain exactly the same ÔÇö Pro does not change how the platform works, it simply removes the word limit*

*What Pro does not change:*

- *The daily 5\-word limit remains in place ÔÇö this is a learning science decision, not a monetization lever, and it applies equally to all users*
- *All ten stages remain mandatory for every word*
- *The 80\-point pass threshold remains the same*
- *No stages are skipped or shortened for Pro users*

__*Why One\-Time Payment*__

*A subscription model would create pressure to keep users dependent on the platform indefinitely\. VocabVault's goal is the opposite ÔÇö to genuinely build a learner's vocabulary until they no longer need the platform\. A one\-time payment aligns the product's incentive with the learner's goal\. The platform succeeds when the learner succeeds, not when they keep paying\.*

__*Plan Indicator on Dashboard*__

*The dashboard displays the learner's current plan clearly at all times\. Free plan users can see their word progress toward the 25\-word limit and a non\-intrusive upgrade prompt\. Pro plan users see a permanent Pro badge on their profile and dashboard with no further prompts or upsells\.*

__*Device Management & Session Security*__

*VocabVault enforces a strict *__*2\-device limit*__* per account\. Every user ÔÇö Free or Pro ÔÇö is allowed to be logged in on a maximum of two devices simultaneously at any given time\. This is a hard system rule with no exceptions\.*

__*How It Works*__

*When a user logs in on a device, that device is registered to their account\. The system tracks active sessions by device fingerprint and session token, stored server\-side\. The account can hold a maximum of two registered active sessions at once\.*

*If a user attempts to log in on a *__*third device*__* while two devices are already active, the system immediately and automatically logs out all existing sessions across every device ÔÇö including the two that were previously active\. The user is then logged in fresh on the new device as the first active session\. No manual action is required from the user, and no warning is given to the previously active devices ÔÇö they are simply signed out and redirected to the login page\.*

*This behavior is intentional\. It discourages account sharing while remaining frictionless for legitimate users who simply switched devices\.*

__*Device Registration*__

*Each registered device is visible to the user in their *__*Profile Settings*__* under a dedicated *__*Active Devices*__* section\. The learner can see:*

- *Device name and type \(browser, mobile, desktop\)*
- *Operating system*
- *Last active timestamp*
- *Location \(city and country based on IP\)*

*The user can manually log out from any registered device remotely at any time from this panel ÔÇö without needing physical access to that device\.*

__*Account Lock*__

*In certain cases, a user's account can be placed in a *__*locked state*__*\. A locked account cannot be accessed from any device until the lock is resolved\.*

*Conditions that trigger an account lock:*

- __*Suspicious login activity*__* ÔÇö multiple rapid login attempts from different geographic locations in a short time window, suggesting credential compromise*
- __*Repeated third\-device login attempts*__* ÔÇö if the system detects a pattern of a third device repeatedly triggering the global logout, the account is flagged and temporarily locked pending identity verification*
- __*Payment dispute or chargeback*__* ÔÇö if a Pro user initiates a chargeback on their one\-time payment, the account is locked immediately pending resolution*
- __*Violation of platform terms*__* ÔÇö accounts reported or detected for sharing credentials, automated bot activity on stages, or any attempt to manipulate scoring are locked pending review*

__*What Happens When an Account Is Locked*__

*The user cannot log in from any device\. On every login attempt, they are shown a clear message explaining that their account has been locked and the reason ÔÇö where possible ÔÇö along with instructions for how to contact support to resolve it\.*

*Active sessions on any currently logged\-in device are terminated immediately at the moment the lock is applied\.*

*The lock is not permanent by default\. Each lock type has a resolution path ÔÇö identity verification, support contact, payment resolution, or manual review ÔÇö after which the account is reinstated and the user regains full access\.*

__*Security Architecture Notes*__

*Device session tracking is handled server\-side via *__*Upstash Redis*__* ÔÇö each active session token is stored with its device metadata and a reference to the user account\. On every authenticated request, the session is validated against Redis before the request proceeds\. If the session has been invalidated \(due to third\-device login or account lock\), the request is rejected and the client is redirected to the login page immediately\.*

*Firebase Authentication handles the token issuance\. The server\-side session validator checks both the Firebase token validity and the Redis session record simultaneously ÔÇö both must be valid for access to be granted\.*
